FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — GST Services Karnataka

Can a GST consultant from another Karnataka city handle my filing? ▾
Yes. GST registration and filing are done entirely online through the GST portal. A Bangalore consultant can handle GST compliance for your business in Mysore, Mangalore, or Hubli without any physical visits. WorkIndex lets you choose based on expertise and price, not geography.
What documents are needed for GST registration in Karnataka? ▾
PAN card, Aadhaar, business registration proof (partnership deed, incorporation certificate, etc.), bank account details, business address proof (electricity bill, rent agreement), and passport-size photographs. For Karnataka businesses, Karnataka-specific trade licences may also be required depending on your business type.
Are GST rates cheaper in Tier 2 Karnataka cities? ▾
Consultant fees (not GST rates — those are fixed by the government) are generally lower in Mysore, Hubli, and Mangalore compared to Bangalore. Since the service is online, you can hire a Bangalore consultant for Tier 2 city expertise, or a local consultant at lower rates. WorkIndex lets you compare both.
What is GSTR-9 and when do Karnataka businesses need to file it? ▾
GSTR-9 is the annual GST return summarising all monthly/quarterly returns for the financial year. It's mandatory for businesses with turnover above ₹2 crore. The deadline is 31 December of the following financial year. Businesses above ₹5 crore also need GSTR-9C (reconciliation statement with auditor certification).
I run a small shop in Karnataka — do I need GST registration? ▾
Only if your annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for service providers in some states). Below this threshold, registration is optional. However, voluntary registration can help you claim ITC on purchases, appear more credible to customers, and avoid registration hassle later when you grow. A GST consultant can advise based on your specific situation.
