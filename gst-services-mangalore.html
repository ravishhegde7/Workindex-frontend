FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions — GST Services Mangalore

I export goods through Mangalore Port — how does GST apply? ▾
Exports are zero-rated under GST. You can either pay IGST and claim a refund later, or export under a Letter of Undertaking (LUT) without paying IGST upfront. LUT filing is done annually. Mangalore GST consultants on WorkIndex handle both options and help you claim your refunds efficiently.
My cashew processing business in Mangalore — what are the GST implications? ▾
Raw cashew nuts attract 5% GST, while processed cashews attract 5% as well. Job work for cashew processing is taxed at 12%. ITC on input services and packing materials can be claimed. Mangalore's cashew exporters can also claim export-related refunds. A local GST consultant familiar with the sector can optimise your compliance.
Does WorkIndex cover Udupi, Puttur, and other Dakshina Kannada areas? ▾
Yes. Mangalore-based GST consultants on WorkIndex serve all of Dakshina Kannada and Udupi districts — Puttur, Bantwal, Sullia, Belthangady, Udupi, Kundapura, and surrounding areas. Since the process is fully online, there's no geographical limitation.
What documents do I need for GST registration in Mangalore? ▾
PAN, Aadhaar, business registration proof, bank account details, business address proof (electricity bill or rent agreement for your Mangalore premises). For exporters, IEC (Import Export Code) is also needed. Your GST consultant will provide a complete checklist based on your business type.
How do I handle IGST paid on imports through Mangalore Port? ▾
IGST paid on imports through customs can be claimed as ITC in your GSTR-3B once the import entry (Bill of Entry) is reflected in your ICEGATE data. This ITC can be used to offset your output GST liability. Reconciliation between GSTR-2A and customs data requires careful handling — a Mangalore GST consultant with import experience is essential.
